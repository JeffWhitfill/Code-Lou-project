Neuro Tai Chi

A moving meditation for patients with a neurological disease to keep healthy

Explanation of Yin Yang Symbol

The white and black represent the balance of life. The whole is greater than either half. You can think of it as gathering energy vs expending energy. The dark side is thought of as the feminine side or gathering side where as the light side is the masculine side and expending energy. The two dots in each figure are to illustrate the presence of the other energy within it. Energy needs to be gathered before it can be expelled.

Styles of Tai Chi (named after the families who invented them)

  • Wu style

Neuro Tai Chi has several rules:

  • The lower and upper half should be coordinated
  • Be in constant motion... but slow
  • Don't bend your knee past your big toe
  • Turns should be done at the hip, not at the ankle or knee

Warmup Exercises - starting in chair

  • Stretch each foot, heal to toe
  • Stretch each foot, side to side
  • Lift each leg individually, rotate ankle
  • Rotate ankles in opposite directions
  • Massage each leg
  • Warm up hands by opening and closing
  • Rotate shoulders forward and then back
  • Hold arms facing you, turn hands and let neck fall forward
  • Rotate at your mid-section 45 degrees
  • Hold imaginary ball in between your hands, turn at the mid-section
  • Alternate raising one arm as if blocking the sun, sit the other low as if placing on a table
  • Hold arms like a paddle wheel and rotate, pushing and pulling
  • Stand and perform 15 quarter squats
  • Rock your energy from leg to leg
  • Alternate balancing on each leg, foot points down

Tai Chi Walk

You may wander why there is a specific Tai Chi walk since we learned to walk when we were about 1 or 2 years old. The problem is we had improper instruction. If you go to a shoe store where they measure feet to fit the shoe/boot perfectly to your feet, they will undoubtedly notice that your current shoe has an uneven tread. For most people, this phenomenon doesn't affect their daily life. For someone with a neurological disease this could cause back pain, hip pain and even a fall.

If you watch someone who has studied Tai Chi for a while, you'll notice that when they walk, their head remains still and doesn't bob up and down. They could walk up a flight of stairs and appear as if they were riding an escalator. They move like a river, flowing from point A to point B without impediment. The goals of practicing a Tai Chi walk is to improve balance and endurance.

The Box Form by Dr.Paul Lam

  1. Stand with legs shoukder-width apart
  2. Bring left leg in and salute (left hand flat on top of your right fist
  3. Return to staning with legs shoukder-width apart, weight balanced between both legs
  4. Bring both arms up, keeping a head-width distance between them and bring in to a foot distance in front of your chest
  5. Take three deep breaths
  6. Shift your weight to your right leg
  7. Wave hands like clouds three times to the left, stepping left as you go
  8. Bring hands in front of chest again and take one breath
  9. Place box down by left hip, on an imaginary tablre, and then slide it to the right side and then off the table
  10. Raise hands diagonally, by left shoulder and then usher the box out the window, turning your right hip as you go
  11. Bring hands in front of chest again and take one breath
  12. Perform steps 9 and 10 again but begin with putting the box by your right hip (replace left with right and vice-versa)
  13. Shift your weight to your left leg
  14. Wave hands like clouds three times to the right, stepping right as you go
  15. Take three deep breaths
  16. Let arms fall to your sides
  17. Bring left leg in and salute (left hand flat on top of your right fist
  18. Return to staning with legs shoukder-width apart, weight balanced between both legs

The Yang 16 Form (a version of the Yang traditional form)

  1. Preparation and beginning
  2. Wave hands like clouds - right
  3. Wave hands like clouds - left
  4. Single Whip - right
  5. Right fist below left elbow
  6. White crane spreads it wings
  7. Left brush knee and push
  8. Step up to strum the lute
  9. Step back with left leg and repulse monkey
  10. Left strike tiger
  11. Part wild horses mane with right hand
  12. Step forward and punch low with right fist
  13. Turn body opposite way and white snake spits out tongue
  14. Step forward and parry(right), block(left) and punch(right)
  15. Step left and grasp bird's tail - ward off left
  16. Step right and grasp bird's tail - ward off right
  17. Grasp bird's tail - roll back left
  18. Grasp bird's tail - press right
  19. Grasp bird's tail - split arms and push
  20. Turn body to starting position and cross hands (double ward off)
  21. Close by dropping hands to your side

With any neurological disease, especially Multiple Sclerosis and Parkinson's disease, it has been proven that movement, like in Tai Chi, reduces the impact of symptoms of the disease. Along with a healthy diet and a positive attitude, practicing Tai Chi even at a beginner level can greatly increase the quality of one's life.
